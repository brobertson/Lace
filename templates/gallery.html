{% filter markdown %} Gallery ------- We are making strides in OCRing Migne's _Patrologia Graeca_: Here is [a text](http://heml.mta.ca/rigaudon/runs/pt3patrologiaecur16mign) processed last year; here is [a similar one](http://heml.mta.ca/rigaudon/runs/patrologiaecursu45mignuoft) processed in 2014. We have many collections of fragments, papyri, etc, such as [Kock, T. _Comicorum Atticorum Fragmenta 3_](http://heml.mta.ca/lace/render_page?hocrtype_id=4804) and Koerte's fragments of [Metrodorus of Lampsacus](http://heml.mta.ca/lace/render_page?hocrtype_id=4754). Complete or near-complete series include the volumes in [Commentaria in Aristotelem Graeca](http://heml.mta.ca/lace/render_page?hocrtype_id=1964), Cramer's [Catena Graecorum Patrum](http://heml.mta.ca/lace/render_page?hocrtype_id=4894), and Meyer's [Critical and Exegetical Handbook](http://heml.mta.ca/lace/sidebysideview2/9426275) series of biblical commentaries. Teachers of ancient Greek might be interested in [Rouse, W. H. D. _A Greek Boy at Home, A Story Written in Greek._](http://heml.mta.ca/lace/render_page?hocrtype_id=5449) or these [Greek](http://heml.mta.ca/lace/render_page?hocrtype_id=3064) [grammars](http://heml.mta.ca/lace/sidebysideview2/7394823). [Lexica](http://heml.mta.ca/lace/render_page?hocrtype_id=6104) and [glossaries](http://heml.mta.ca/lace/render_page?hocrtype_id=5718) have great potential for data-mining. Unusal fonts processed include this [Bekker edition of Aristotle](http://heml.mta.ca/lace/render_page?hocrtype_id=2168), published by Reimer. The **Loeb** editions provide facing translations, useful for natural language processing.
